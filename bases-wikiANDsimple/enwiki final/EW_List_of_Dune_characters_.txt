
"Dune" is a science fiction media franchise that originated with the 1965 novel "Dune" by American author Frank Herbert. "Dune" is frequently cited as the best-selling science fiction novel in history, and won the 1966 Hugo Award as well as the inaugural Nebula Award for Best Novel. Herbert wrote five sequels before his death in 1986. "Dune" was adapted into a 1984 film, and a 2000 television miniseries. The first two sequels were presented as a miniseries in 2003.
Since 1999, Herbert's son Brian Herbert and science fiction author Kevin J. Anderson have published eleven prequel novels, as well as two which complete the original "Dune" series.
Frank Herbert works.
Frank Herbert's "Dune" was published in 1965, and he wrote five sequels before his death in 1986: "Dune Messiah" (1969), "Children of Dune" (1976), "God Emperor of Dune" (1981), "Heretics of Dune" (1984), and "" (1985). "Dune" follows Paul, the scion of House Atreides, as his family is thrown into the dangerous political intrigues centered on the desert planet Arrakis, only known source of the oracular spice melange, the most important and valuable substance in the universe. The series spans 5,000 years, focusing on Paul's various descendants.
Brian Herbert and Kevin J. Anderson works.
Since 1999, Herbert's son Brian Herbert and author Kevin J. Anderson have published eleven prequel novels, collected in the series "Prelude to Dune" (1999–2001), "Legends of Dune" (2002–2004), "Heroes of Dune" (2008–2009), and "Great Schools of Dune" (2012–2016). They have also released two novels—"Hunters of Dune" (2006) and "Sandworms of Dune" (2007)—which complete the original series.
