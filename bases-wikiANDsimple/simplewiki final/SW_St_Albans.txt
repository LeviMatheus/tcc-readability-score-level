St Albans is the main urban area of the City and District of St Albans in southern Hertfordshire, England.
Location.
St Albans is found in southern Hertfordshire, England, 22 km north of central London.
History.
It was originally named "Verlamion" by the Ancient British, Catuvellauni tribe. It was the first major town on the old Roman road of Watling Street for travellers heading north and became the Roman city of Verulamium.
