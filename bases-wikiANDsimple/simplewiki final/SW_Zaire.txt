Zaire (spelled Za√Øre in French) used to be the name of the Democratic Republic of the Congo. It began to use the name Zaire in the year 1971, and it stopped in 1997. It was named Zaire for most of the time when Mobutu Sese Seko was dictator of the country. The kind of money of Zaire was also called the zaire.
Origin of the name.
The name 'Zaire' came from a Portuguese corruption of the Kikongo word 'nzere', meaning 'river'.
